Gilbert Padilla is an American labor who, along with Cesar Chavez & Dolores Huerta, founded the UFW

His passion, personal sacrifice & commitment to farm worker social justice significantly contributed to the creation & fruition of the 1st successful farm workers' union in America

"In September of 1973, the UFW conducted its 1st Constitutional Convention in Fresno, CA, and I was elected Secretary-Treasurer"

Sen. Edward M. Kennedy championed the cause of the UFW after picking up the mantle from his brother Sen. Robert F. Kennedy, following his assassination in 1968

Sen. Ted Kennedy with Dr. Gilbert Padilla at an UFW event in Delano, CA

The UFW empowered farmworkers to help improve their working conditions & wages, and embraces nonviolence in its attempt to cultivate members on political and social issues

The UFW continues to actively champion legislative & regulatory reforms for farm workers covering issues such as pesticides, overtime, heat safety and other worker protections

Origin Story

Gilbert was born in Los Banos, California. His parents had moved there only two years prior. The family lived in a labor camp called San Juan Camp and, consequently, Padilla spent his early years watching his parents work in the fields. During World War II, Padilla and his brothers enlisted in the army, where he served with the U.S. Army, 1st Cavalry Division in Japan

Today


Gilbert lives with his daughter, Adelita, in Fresno, CA, a short walk from BITWISE HQ. His wife Esther passed away in 2013. He continues to be busy as ever, leading an active life with visiting family & friends from worldwide











Former "huelgistas" & "compadres" visit and stay in touch with Gilbert regularly, he is an information resource and always makes time for individuals interested in the farmworker struggle and history
